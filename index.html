Switzerland
Switzerland, officially the Swiss Confederation, is a landlocked country in Central Europe renowned for its Alpine mountains, political neutrality, and high standard of living. It consistently ranks among the wealthiest and most stable nations globally, with the third-highest GDP per capita in the world.
Finland
Finland, officially the Republic of Finland, is a Nordic country in Northern Europe known for its vast forests, thousands of lakes, and high quality of life. It consistently ranks as the happiest country in the world and features a unique blend of modern technology and pristine nature.
Ireland
Ireland, officially the Republic of Ireland, is an island nation in Northwestern Europe known for its lush green landscapes, rich literary history, and rapid economic transformation. Often called the "Celtic Tiger" during its boom years, it remains one of the wealthiest countries in the EU by GDP per capita, though this is heavily influenced by multinational corporate activity.
Australia
Australia, officially the Commonwealth of Australia, is a sovereign country comprising the mainland of the Australian continent, the island of Tasmania, and numerous smaller islands. It is the sixth-largest country by area and is renowned for its unique biodiversity, vast outback, and high quality of life.
Netherland
Netherlands, often informally called Holland, is a Northwestern European country known for its flat landscape, extensive canal systems, and liberal social policies. As of June 2026, it is governed by the Jetten cabinet, a historic minority coalition formed in February 2026.
Germany
Germany, officially the Federal Republic of Germany, is a Central European nation and the largest economy in Europe. As of June 2026, it is undergoing a significant political and economic transformation under a new conservative-led government, shifting away from the previous "traffic light" coalition's policies to address stagnation and security challenges.
